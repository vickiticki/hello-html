Leonardo da Vinci

Introduction

Leonardo di Ser Piero da Vinci was born in Vinci, a small town outside Florence, on April 15, 1452. He was the illegitimate son of a notary, Piero da Vinci, and a peasant, Caterina di Meo Lippi. He lived during the Italian High Renaissance. He was a popular artist in his day, and since his death has become one of the most famous artists in history. He created two of the most famous paintings in the world, Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. His Salvator Mundi (as of this writing) holds the record for most expensive painting ever sold at a public auction. Of course, he is also very well known for his numerous unfinished projects.

But Leonardo was far more than just a painter. In fact, he finished rather few paintings compared to other artists of his era, mostly due to the fact he was constantly distracted by other things. His list of jobs included:

  • Sculptor
  • Inventor
  • Engineer
  • Architect
  • Musician
  • Scientist
  • Anatomist
  • Geologist
  • and more...
